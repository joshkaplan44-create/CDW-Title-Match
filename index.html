Turning 790,000 Job Title Variations Into a Single Source of Truth

CDW's contact database contained over 790,000 unique, uncleaned job title strings — free-text entries riddled with abbreviations, typos, phone numbers, and inconsistent formatting. This project standardized every title against Zoom's canonical persona schema, creating a clean, structured foundation for the Golden Contact Identifier (GCI) — CDW's enterprise identity spine for resolving contacts into trusted golden records across all systems.

Why Title Standardization Matters for Identity Resolution

A golden record — a single, trusted, deduplicated view of a contact — is only as reliable as its component fields. Job title is one of the most identity-defining attributes in B2B data, yet it is also one of the most inconsistently captured. When titles like "Sr. Dir IT Ops", "Sr Director, IT Operations", and "Senior Director of Information Technology Operations" all refer to the same persona, systems that rely on raw strings will treat them as three different people.

Standardizing to a canonical schema solves this by giving every contact a normalized title signal that can be used for deduplication, segmentation, propensity modeling, and persona-based targeting — regardless of how the original title was entered.

What We Did

1
Built a canonical schema by combining Zoom's Job Function and Management Level fields into 745 structured titles spanning 148 functions and 5 seniority levels.
2
Vectorized all 790K raw CDW titles using TF-IDF to represent each title mathematically based on the importance of its component words.
3
Phase 1 — matched each title via cosine similarity, assigning the best-fitting canonical title to every record scoring ≥ 60% (267,816 records, 33.9%). Records below threshold were not discarded — they were passed to Phase 2.
4
Phase 2 — applied a hierarchy keyword classifier to the 522,410 Phase 1 NEI records, recovering an additional 58,737 (7.4%) into Zoom's management-level tiers (Manager, Director, VP-Level, C-Level, Board Members). Combined coverage: 326,553 records (41.3%).

How We Built the Canonical Schema

A 3-step pipeline to collapse 790,100 messy titles into 745 clean, structured Zoom personas
1
Construct the Canonical Title List from Zoom Fields
Zoom's data schema exposes two structured persona fields: Job Function and Management Level. We concatenated these two fields — e.g. "Information Technology" + "Director" → "Information Technology Director" — to produce a clean grid of standardized titles. The list was then filtered down to the 148 job functions most relevant to CDW's B2B technology audience, yielding 745 canonical titles spanning 5 seniority levels.
2
Vectorize All Titles with TF-IDF
Every CDW raw title and every canonical title was converted into a TF-IDF vector (Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency) — a mathematical representation that weights each word by how distinctive it is across the full title corpus. Common words like "of" or "the" receive low weight; meaningful terms like "Security", "DevOps", or "Director" receive high weight. This produced a 790,100-row sparse matrix that captured the semantic content of each title numerically.
3
Match via Cosine Similarity (≥ 60% threshold)
Each raw CDW title vector was compared against all 745 canonical title vectors using cosine similarity — measuring the angle between vectors to determine how closely two titles align in meaning regardless of exact wording. The best-scoring canonical title was assigned if its similarity score met or exceeded a 60% threshold. Titles scoring below 60% were marked Not Enough Information (NEI). The entire 790K-record match ran in 8.9 seconds via optimised sparse matrix operations.

From Analysis to Operation

Operationalizing Title Standardization Inside the GCI Pipeline

This matching exercise produced a validated standardization table — but the real value is realized when that table becomes a permanent, automated step inside CDW's Golden Contact Identifier (GCI) pipeline, so that every new contact entering the system is standardized at ingestion rather than after the fact.

What is the GCI?
The Golden Contact Identifier (GCI) is CDW's enterprise identity spine — an abstraction layer that resolves the same individual across CRM, marketing platforms, digital interactions, events, and analytics into a single, trusted canonical identity. It does not replace Salesforce or any operational system; it provides the connective tissue that links records across systems, ensuring that downstream analytics, personas, and activation always operate against the same understanding of who a contact is. Title standardization is a critical input to this process — a clean, structured job title dramatically improves identity resolution accuracy, persona assignment, and buying committee modeling.

The Path Forward
1
Publish the Standardization Lookup Table
Ready Now
The matching output already exists as a structured table: raw CDW title → canonical Zoom title → match score. This needs to be formalized as a versioned, governed lookup asset — a flat table (or database view) that maps every matched raw title string to its canonical equivalent. This table becomes the foundation for all downstream standardization logic and should be stored in the same data layer as other GCI reference assets.
267,816 matched rows ready to export Fields: raw_title, canonical_title, match_score, department, job_function, management_level
2
Integrate into GCI Source Ingestion & Standardization (Phase 1)
Next Step
The GCI lifecycle's first phase — Source Ingestion & Standardization — is where incoming contact records are cleaned and normalized before entering identity resolution. The title standardization table should be applied here as a pre-resolution enrichment step: when a new CDW contact record is ingested with a raw job title string, the pipeline performs a table lookup and, if a match exists, appends the canonical title, department, job function, and management level as structured attributes on the record. This happens before identity resolution, which means the IDR engine has clean persona signals to work with from the start.
GCI Design Principle — Standardize at Ingestion, Not Downstream
GCI's architecture is explicit: source data is made comparable and usable at ingestion time, without overwriting the original field. The raw title is preserved for lineage; the canonical title is added as a governed, derived attribute. This keeps identity logic clean and prevents downstream tools from re-solving a problem that should only be solved once.
3
Address the Remaining ~59% (NEI Records)
Iterative
The 463,673 records below the 60% confidence threshold are not lost — they represent a solvable quality problem. Strategies for closing this gap over time include:
Threshold Tuning
Lower the 60% threshold selectively for high-confidence function categories, or apply tiered thresholds by department to recover more matches without sacrificing quality.
ZoomInfo Enrichment
For NEI records with sufficient identity signals, use ZoomInfo's enrichment API to retrieve a standardized title directly — bypassing the fuzzy match entirely for records where ZI has coverage.
Manual Review Queue
Surface the highest-frequency NEI raw titles (those appearing 100+ times) for a targeted human review pass. A small effort on the most common patterns yields disproportionate coverage gains.
Schema Expansion
Some NEI titles may represent legitimate CDW-specific roles not covered by the current 745 Zoom titles. Identify clusters and propose additions to the canonical schema in coordination with the ZoomInfo contract.
4
Unlock Downstream GCI Capabilities
Downstream Value
Once canonical titles are flowing through the GCI pipeline at ingestion, they become a structured signal available to every downstream system and capability — without any additional integration work per platform:
Persona Intelligence
Department + Job Function + Management Level from the canonical schema feed directly into GCI's persona model, enabling structured audience segments without manual classification.
Buying Committee Modeling
Standardized management levels allow GCI to identify decision-makers, influencers, and end-users within an account — a prerequisite for account-based marketing and PMAI.
Identity Resolution Quality
A clean canonical title dramatically improves probabilistic matching accuracy — two records with the same name, company, and canonical title are far stronger merge candidates than those with mismatched free-text strings.
MARKO & AI Orchestration
Structured persona attributes enable AI-assisted orchestration tools like MARKO to make role-aware content and timing decisions without ad-hoc title parsing at the application layer.
