💡 Key Business Insights
What this data tells us: CDW's contact base of 790k records spans technology buyers, finance leaders, operations managers, and executive decision-makers. The matching pipeline identified 267,816 high-confidence personas (33.9%), with an additional 58,737 hierarchy-classified contacts recovered from the NEI pool — giving you meaningful seniority intelligence on 41.3% of the total contact universe.
💻
IT Dominates the Contact Base
47.1%
Information Technology accounts for 125,823 matched contacts — nearly half the entire matched population. This reflects CDW's core positioning as a technology solutions provider, where IT buyers are the primary audience. Manager (87K) and Director (31K) density here is unmatched by any other department.
💰
Finance: Rich in Director-Level Contacts
26.8%
Finance has a Director ratio of 26.8% — higher than the cross-department average of ~27.8%. With 7,575 Directors and 3,622 VP-Level contacts, Finance is the second-richest department for senior outreach, ideal for CFO/FP&A product positioning such as cloud finance tools and software licensing.
🏛
C-Suite Dept Has Unusual Manager Density
12,435
The "C-Suite / Executive" department contains 12,435 Manager-level contacts — counterintuitively high. This suggests many contacts use executive-adjacent titles (e.g., "Executive Operations Manager") that map to the C-Suite function. These are high-influence gatekeepers worth prioritizing for outreach.
⚖️
Legal & Compliance: Elevated VP Ratio
13.0%
Legal & Compliance has a VP-level penetration of 13.0% (811 VPs out of 6,237) — one of the highest VP ratios across all departments. Combined with 2,361 Directors, this makes Legal a surprisingly strong channel for senior-level compliance technology and governance solution conversations.
🏥
Medical & Health: Small But Senior
1.8%
Medical & Health represents only 1.8% of matched records (4,856), but features a strong Manager/Director blend (2,603 Mgr / 1,716 Dir). For CDW Healthcare, this sector presents high revenue-per-contact potential. The relatively small universe also suggests significant upside in list expansion for this vertical.
📬
522K NEI Records: Recoverable Goldmine
88,504
Of the 522,410 "Not Enough Information" records, 88,504 scored 50–59% — just below the confidence threshold. These are high-recovery candidates that warrant manual review or a ZoomInfo enrichment pass. Lowering the threshold to 55% would recover ~20-25% more contacts without significantly reducing quality.
